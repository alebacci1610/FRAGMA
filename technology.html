FIG 01 // OPERATIONAL STACK

Multidimensional Context

The operational architecture of Fragma is not linear; it is vertical. We have mapped the battlefield of reality into a stack of three interlinked planes: the Cognitive, the Virtual, and the Physical. This isometric structure allows for granular control of the perceived environment, where each layer feeds into the next in a continuous recursive loop.

The Cognitive Plane (Levels 1-2) represents the highest order of abstraction. This is where semantic structures, psychological biases, and cultural narratives reside. It is the domain of strategic intent and consensus engineering. Here, we do not manipulate data, but the meaning assigned to data.

The Virtual Plane acts as the transport layer, utilizing proprietary algorithms to manage signal distribution across digital vectors. Finally, the Physical Plane anchors the system, encompassing the hardware, the server farms, and ultimately, the biological interface: the human user.

FIG 02 // NEURAL INTERFACE

Adaptive Tiered Neuromodulation

The documented system presents a highly complex Brain-Computer Interface (BCI) structured on a hierarchical scale of five operational levels, defined as Tiers (0-4). This progression is strictly regulated by physical precision parameters and intensity measured in Watts per centimeter (W-cm).

While lower levels (Tier 1) operate in a low-frequency regime (30Hz) for surface-level suggestion, the critical levels such as Tier 4 utilize invasive deep neural stimulation reaching frequencies of 100Hz. This capability allows the system to rewrite processing in Broca's Area in real-time.

The integration of these frequencies aims to manipulate specific neuro-linguistic circuits through an adaptive neuromodulation protocol. This effectively reaches the soft tissue level of the brain with a precision estimated at approximately 100µm, allowing for the editing of semantic intent before it becomes conscious thought.
